Design Brief: The Gold Standard Glamping Landing Page
1. Project Overview
Objective: Create a high-conversion, mobile-first landing page for premium UK farm glamping.

**Important to note - Production build: desktop-first layout with responsive breakpoints; mobile UX requirements below still apply.**

Core Philosophy: "Organic Luxury." The site must feel as cozy as a wool blanket but function as efficiently as a top-tier hotel booking engine.

Primary Action: Direct booking (or lead capture for top-of-funnel users).

2. Visual Identity & Aesthetic
Palette: Earthy and sophisticated. Forest greens, slate greys, burnt orange/terracotta accents, and "parchment" off-whites (avoid #FFFFFF).

Typography: A mix of a classic Serif for headers (e.g., Lora or Playfair Display) to feel established and "country," paired with a clean Sans-Serif for body text (e.g., Montserrat or Open Sans) for legibility.

Imagery: Heavy use of "lifestyle" shots—smoke rising from a chimney, close-ups of local farm produce, and wide-angle drone shots of the UK countryside.

3. Page Structure & UI Modules
Section 1: The Functional Hero (Above the Fold)
Visual: Full-bleed hero image/video with a subtle dark overlay for text contrast.

The "Anchor" (Booking Widget): A horizontal bar (Desktop) or a centered card (Mobile).

Inputs: Check-in, Check-out, Guests.

CTA: High-contrast "Check Availability" button (e.g., a warm copper or deep gold color).

Lead Gen Micro-copy: A small link below the CTA: "Download our 'Hidden Farm Walks' guide for 10% off your stay."

Section 2: The "At a Glance" Feature Grid
UI Style: 3x2 or 4x2 grid of custom line-art icons.

Required Icons: Wood-fired Hot Tub, King Size Bed, Luxury Ensuite, Private Firepit, Pet Friendly, Farm-to-Table Breakfast.

UX Note: No long descriptions. Just the icon and a 2-3 word label.

Section 3: The "Curated Stays" (Accommodation Choice)
UI Style: Large, "Polaroid" style cards for each unit (e.g., The Shepherd’s Hut vs. The Safari Tent).

Mobile UX: A horizontal swipe-able carousel to save vertical space.

Price Transparency: Clearly display "From £XXX / night" to qualify leads immediately.

Section 4: Social Proof & Trust
Google/TripAdvisor Integration: A "Trust Bar" featuring 5-star ratings and a few "Verified Guest" snippets.

Authenticity Badge: A "Working Farm" badge to signify the authentic UK experience.

Section 5: The "Farm Life" Interactive Map (Optional but Recommended)
Visual: A simplified, illustrated map of the property.

UX: Interactive hotspots that show the distance between units to reassure guests about privacy.

4. Technical & UX Requirements
Mobile-First Design: 80% of users will be on mobile. The "Book Now" button should become a sticky footer once the user scrolls past the Hero section.

Fast Loading: Optimize all high-res country imagery; use WebP formats to ensure the "escape" doesn't feel like a "wait."

Accessibility: Ensure high contrast for text on "earthy" backgrounds (WCAG 2.1 compliance).

5. The "Hook" (Lead Magnet Popup/Slide-in)
Trigger: Exit-intent or 30 seconds on page.

Offer: "Join the Farm Club: Be the first to hear about seasonal lambing weekends and late-availability discounts."

Designer's Deliverables
High-fidelity Figma/Adobe XD mockups (Desktop & Mobile).

Custom Icon Set (consistent stroke weight, rustic style).

UI Kit (Typography scales, buttons, form states).
